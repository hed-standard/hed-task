# Cross-Reference: 78-Task Catalog vs. Cognitive Atlas

This document maps each of our 78 curated tasks to its counterpart(s) in the Cognitive Atlas (cognitiveontology.org). The Atlas contains 849 task entries organized into 366 groups. This cross-reference identifies exact matches, close matches, related entries, and gaps in both directions.

## Match Type Key

- **exact** — The Atlas contains an entry that is unambiguously the same paradigm (same name, same procedure).
- **close** — The Atlas has an entry for the same paradigm family but under a slightly different name, a specific variant, or a broader/narrower scope (e.g., Atlas has 'Penn Continuous Performance Task' rather than generic CPT).
- **related** — The Atlas has entries in the same domain but no single entry that corresponds to our task. Our task maps to a group of Atlas entries without a clear one-to-one match (e.g., our 'Face Processing / FFA Localizer' maps to the Atlas 'face_processing_tasks' group but no single entry is a direct match).
- **none** — The Atlas contains no entry for this paradigm.

## Tasks Absent from the Cognitive Atlas

These 8 tasks have no representation in the Cognitive Atlas (849 entries). Several are notable paradigms with substantial literatures:

1. **Task 41: Affective Priming Task**
   Fazio et al. (1986) paradigm for automatic evaluation of valence. Widely used in social cognition. The Atlas has an Implicit Association Task entry but no affective priming task.

1. **Task 47: Mismatch Negativity Paradigm**
   The MMN is one of the most studied ERP components in cognitive neuroscience and clinical populations (schizophrenia, language, development). Its absence is a significant gap in the Atlas.

1. **Task 58: Multiple Object Tracking**
   Pylyshyn & Storm (1988) paradigm; foundational for visual attention research. Absent despite being a standard paradigm in attention labs.

1. **Task 60: Dot-Probe Task**
   MacLeod, Mathews, & Tata (1986) paradigm; the dominant paradigm in anxiety and attentional-bias research. A notable clinical-research gap.

1. **Task 68: Useful Field of View (UFOV)**
   Ball & Owsley UFOV; widely used in aging, driving fitness, and clinical neuropsychology research.

1. **Task 71: Mnemonic Discrimination Task**
   Stark lab mnemonic similarity/discrimination task; the leading paradigm for testing hippocampal pattern separation in humans.

1. **Task 75: Social Incentive Delay (SID)**
   The social analog of the Monetary Incentive Delay task; used in autism, social motivation, and developmental research.

1. **Task 78: Weapons Identification Task (Shooter Bias)**
   Payne (2001) / Correll et al. (2002) paradigm; the leading experimental measure of implicit racial bias in decision-making.
